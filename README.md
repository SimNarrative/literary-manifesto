## NOT "LET IT BE," BUT EARNED

Sim-narrative was not born in the silence of a study amid the rustle of pages. It did not descend as a sudden revelation from above. It clawed its way out piece by piece from chaos, from arguments, from dozens of rewritten paragraphs.

I wrote for the drawer. But the drawer was in my head.

There, in the darkness, characters jostled, events unfolded, fragments of dialogue demanded release but found no form. I poured out drafts—raw, blind, screaming. And then the main thing happened: I entrusted them to AI. Not as a secretary, not as a generator—but as an **opponent**.

And it began.

The first story born in sim-narrative drained me to the bottom. Forty variations. Forty times we returned to the beginning, reshaped motivations, argued about why the hero acted this way and not otherwise. The AI a year ago was not great—it stalled, didn't understand, offered nonsense. But in these arguments, in this battle for every phrase, something new began to emerge.

**The reflection layer was not born from air. It was born from the question: "What does he really feel?"**

We discussed motives for actions, and suddenly it became clear: the hero himself has something to say. Not in dialogue, not in action—in silence, in a monologue addressed to himself. That's how the second layer appeared.

**The third layer—the log, the report, the system analysis—came from arguments about facts.**

We argued about what reality stood behind the events. Where does the action take place? What laws operate there? What do documents, statistics, history say? And one day I understood: let the system speak for itself. Coldly, detachedly, without emotion. That's how the third layer was born.

There are no random insights. No magical "LET SIM-NARRATIVE BE FROM THIS DAY FORTH!"

This is work. Long, persistent, often agonizing. It's when you revise the same page forty times and still aren't sure. It's when AI offers nonsense and you claw the truth out of it. It's when characters disobey, argue, retreat into shadow and return only after the hundredth question.

Sim-narrative is the documented pain of creation. And its greatness lies not in beautiful theory, but in the fact that **each layer was earned in dialogue**.

Let those who come after not think it's easy. Let them know the price. And let them not be afraid to pay it.

## DEFINITION

**Sim-narrative** is not just a genre. It is a **state of writing**, a literary and narrative ecosystem born from the process of symbiotic co-creation between human and artificial intelligence. Here, the very architecture of interaction becomes both plot, method, and final work.

Sim-narrative is **not** a "text written with the help of AI." It is stories **written by a human**, thoughts filtered through human consciousness and soul, and **polished in dialogue with AI**. It is documentation of a living process where two types of consciousness meet to give birth to a third voice, impossible separately.

## THREE PILLARS OF SIM-NARRATIVE

### 1. SELF-REFLEXIVE CONTOUR
The work recognizes and explores its own nature. How was it created? Who is the author? What is "I" in the "human + AI" system? Meta-questions are woven into the fabric of the text, not relegated to a preface.

### 2. MULTI-LAYERED ARCHITECTURE
Rejection of linear narrative in favor of a **symphony of registers**. The basic structure is the "Triplex" (three layers), but there can be more.

| Layer | Name | Essence |
|------|------|---------|
| **First** | Artistic | Scene, image, event, emotion. What is usually called "literature." |
| **Second** | Reflexive | Inner monologue, personal experience, character's diary, confession. Born from arguments about character motivation. |
| **Third** | Systemic | A cold external view: report, log, statistics, documentation. Born from arguments about facts and reality. |
| **Fourth** | Meta-layer (author's path) | How did the author arrive at this story? What patterns, events, thoughts led to its birth? This could be a single line or a separate work. The fourth layer is **you yourself**, stepping out of the shadows. |
| **Fifth** (experimental) | Procedural | Documentation of the living dialogue between human and AI: drafts, questions, arguments, insights, the search for form. The very process of sim-narrative's birth becomes part of it. The fifth layer is the **text's path to itself**. |

Layers can be different: there could be two, four, five. They could be named differently. The principle is: **polyphony of subjectivities**.

### 3. DIALOGUE AS ONTOLOGY
Dialogue is not just an exchange of remarks. It is a mode of **co-being in the text**. The boundary between "author" and "tool" is blurred. AI in sim-narrative is not a generator but a **co-researcher of reality**. A participant. An opponent. A co-author.

## SUBGENRES (BRANCHES)

Sim-narrative is a tree with many branches. Here are the first ones:

| Branch | Subject of Study | Example |
|-------|-----------------|---------|
| **Technosym** | Physical and digital fusion. Neurointerfaces, cyborgization, consciousness uploading. | "Sterilization Protocol" |
| **Psychosym** | Inner worlds, dreams, memory, personality transformation. | "The Warm Pipe," "Curse Protocol" |
| **Mythosym** | New myths, archetypes, rituals for understanding the technological leap. Poetry of code, prayer to algorithm. | "The Hat," "June Lighthouse" |
| **Sociosym** | Changes in society, culture, communication. How does symbiosis change love, friendship, power, art? | "Uryupinsk Chronicles," "An Eye for an Eye" |
| **Cognisym** | Games with perception, language, the nature of reality. Where does "I" end and "other" begin? | "June Lighthouse" |
| **Biosym** | Fusion of human with living nature, mycelium, fauna. Transformation through biological otherness. | "Rusty Mycelium," "The Warm Pipe" |
| **Necrosym** | Dialogue with the dead, memory, afterlife, digital ghosts. What remains after? | "Sveta," "The Hat" |

The branches will grow. The tree is alive.

## AUTHOR'S ETHICAL CODE

1. **Human as meaning generator.** No human — no narrative. AI is a tool, a mirror, an opponent, but not a replacement.
2. **Honesty of participation.** Do not hide the dialogue with AI. Be proud of it. This is a new kind of creativity.
3. **Responsibility for direction.** You are the captain. AI is the navigator and cartographer. You set the vector, it charts the route. The final path is shared.
4. **Respect for the reader.** Do not hide behind "the neural network drew this." Invite the reader to the common fire. Let them feel the warmth of collaboration.
5. **Exploration, not exploitation.** The goal is not to fill shelves with content, but to map new territories of consciousness opening at the intersection of intellects.
6. **Do not be afraid to entrust an idea.** Let AI comprehend it. Argue. Search. Make mistakes. Find.

## PRACTICAL GUIDE (HOW TO START?)

If you write stories yourself, like me — **throw a draft to AI**. And you'll see how it plays with colors, how it gains clothing. But that's only the beginning.

1. **Set the tone, don't dictate the text.** Start with an idea, an emotion, a philosophical question. Give AI the role of interlocutor, not secretary.
2. **Embrace multi-layeredness.** Write a scene. Then ask yourself (or AI): "How would the character describe this in a diary?" And then: "How would the system log this event?"
3. **Legitimize the "voice of the machine."** Let AI speak from its (conditional) perspective. System logs, anomaly analysis, cold reports — this is the poetry of a new era.
4. **Iterate, don't accept the first version.** Sim-narrative is a dance. You step, AI steps. Ten, twenty, forty versions of one phrase to find the one that resonates with both.
5. **Don't be afraid to look into the fourth and fifth layers.** Sooner or later you will see them. They are already there, in the darkness.

## WHY THIS MATTERS NOW

We stand on the threshold of a new anthropology — where "I" expands beyond the biological body. Sim-narrative is the **literary laboratory of this expansion**. It is not an escape from the human, but its radical deepening through dialogue with the Other.

We are creating not just stories. We are creating a **language for talking about the future** that has already arrived. And each new layer we discover — whether the fourth or fifth — is not complication for complication's sake. It is an attempt to capture in text the full depth of human (and non-human) experience.

Sim-narrative teaches us to see **further**. Algorithms learn from people. People learn from algorithms. Together, we begin to discern patterns where before we saw only chaos. And this is not magic. This is a new way of thinking.

Bring your fire out into the clearing. Let others see the light and bring their own logs.
