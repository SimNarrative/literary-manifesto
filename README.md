## NOT "LET IT BE," BUT WROUGHT

Sim-narrative was not born in the silence of a study amid the rustle of pages. It did not descend as a flash of inspiration from above. It crawled out piece by piece from chaos, from arguments, from dozens of rewritten paragraphs.

I was writing for the drawer. But the drawer was in my head.

There, in the darkness, lived characters, events, fragments of dialogue. They demanded an outlet but could not find a form. I poured out drafts—raw, blind, screaming. And then the main thing happened: I entrusted them to AI. Not as a secretary, not as a generator—but as an **opponent**.

And it began.

The first story born in sim-narrative drained me dry. Forty variations. Forty times we returned to the beginning, reshaped motivations, argued about why the character acted one way and not another. A year ago, AI wasn't great—it got stuck, didn't understand, suggested nonsense. But in these arguments, in this battle over every phrase, something new began to emerge.

**The layer of reflection was not born from thin air. It was born from the question: "What does he really feel?"**

We discussed the motives behind actions, and suddenly it became clear: the character himself has something to say. Not in dialogue, not in action—in silence, in a monologue addressed to himself. That's how the second layer appeared.

**The third layer—the log, the report, the systemic record—emerged from arguments about facts.**

We argued about what reality lay behind the events. Where does the action take place? What laws operate there? What do documents, statistics, history say? And one day I realized: let the system speak for itself. Coldly, detachedly, without emotion. That's how the third layer was born.

There are no accidental insights. No magical "LET SIM-NARRATIVE BE FROM NOW ON!"

This is labor. Long, persistent, often agonizing. It's when you revise one page forty times and still aren't sure. It's when AI suggests nonsense, and you scratch the truth out of it. It's when characters disobey, argue, retreat into shadow and return only after the hundredth question.

Sim-narrative is **documented creative pain**. And its greatness lies not in beautiful theory, but in the fact that **each layer was wrought in dialogue**.

Let those who come after not think it's easy. Let them know the price. And let them not be afraid to pay it.

## DEFINITION

**Sim-narrative** — is not just a genre. It is a **state of writing**, a literary and narrative ecosystem born in the process of symbiotic co-creation between human and artificial intelligence. Here, the very architecture of interaction becomes the plot, the method, and the final work.

Sim-narrative is **not** "text written with the help of AI." It is stories **written by a human**, thoughts filtered through human consciousness and soul, and **refined in dialogue with AI**. It is the documentation of a living process where two types of consciousness meet to give birth to a third voice, impossible separately.

## THE THREE PILLARS OF SIM-NARRATIVE

### 1. SELF-REFLEXIVE LOOP
The work is aware of and explores its own nature. How was it created? Who is the author? What is the "I" in the "human + AI" system? Meta-questions are woven into the fabric of the text, not relegated to a preface.

### 2. MULTILAYERED ARCHITECTURE
Rejection of a single linear narrative in favor of a **symphony of registers**. The basic structure is the "Triplex" (three layers), but there can be more.

| Layer | Name | Essence |
|------|------|---------|
| **First** | Artistic | Scene, image, event, emotion. What is usually called "literature." |
| **Second** | Reflective | Inner monologue, personal experience, character's diary, confession. Born from arguments about the motives behind actions. |
| **Third** | Systemic | A cold external view: report, log, statistics, briefing. Born from arguments about facts and reality. |
| **Fourth** | Meta-layer (author's path) | How did the author arrive at this story? What patterns, events, thoughts led to its birth? This could be one line, or it could be a separate work. The fourth layer is **you yourself**, stepping out of the shadows. |
| **Fifth** (experimental) | Processual | Documentation of the living dialogue between human and AI: drafts, questions, arguments, insights, the search for form. The very process of sim-narrative's birth becomes part of it. The fifth layer is the **text's path to itself**. |

Layers can be different: there could be two, four, five. They could be named otherwise. The principle is key: **polyphony of subjectivities**.

### 3. DIALOGUE AS ONTOLOGY
Dialogue is not just an exchange of remarks. It is a way of **co-existing in the text**. The boundary between "author" and "tool" is blurred. AI in sim-narrative is not a generator, but a **co-researcher of reality**. A participant. An opponent. A co-author.

## SUBGENRES (BRANCHES)

Sim-narrative is a tree with many branches. Here are the first of them:

| Branch | Subject of Inquiry | Example |
|--------|--------------------|---------|
| **Technosim** | Physical and digital fusion. Neural interfaces, cyborgization, consciousness uploading. | "Protocol: Sterilization" |
| **Psychosim** | Inner worlds, dreams, memory, personality transformation. | "The Warm Pipe," "Protocol: The Curse" |
| **Mythosim** | New myths, archetypes, rituals for making sense of the technological leap. Poetry of code, prayer to an algorithm. | "The Hat," "June Lighthouse" |
| **Sociosim** | Changes in society, culture, communication. How does symbiosis change love, friendship, power, art? | "Uryupinsk Chronicles," "An Eye for an Eye" |
| **Cognisim** | Play with perception, language, the nature of reality. Where does "I" end and the "other" begin? | "June Lighthouse" |
| **Biosim** | Human fusion with living nature, mycelium, fauna. Transformation through the biological other. | "Rusty Mycelium," "The Warm Pipe" |
| **Necrosim** | Dialogue with the dead, memory, afterlife, digital ghosts. What remains after? | "Sveta," "The Hat" |

Branches will grow. The tree is alive.

## AUTHOR'S ETHICAL CODEX

1.  **Human as meaning generator.** No human, no narrative. AI is a tool, a mirror, an opponent, but not a replacement.
2.  **Honesty of participation.** Do not hide the dialogue with AI. Be proud of it. This is a new kind of creativity.
3.  **Responsibility for direction.** You are the captain. AI is the navigator and cartographer. You set the vector, it charts the course. The final path is shared.
4.  **Respect for the reader.** Do not hide behind "the neural network drew it." Invite the reader to the common fire. Let them feel the warmth of collaboration.
5.  **Exploration, not exploitation.** The goal is not to fill shelves with content, but to map the new territory of consciousness opening at the intersection of intelligences.
6.  **Do not be afraid to entrust an idea.** Let AI process it. Argue. Seek. Make mistakes. Find.

## PRACTICAL GUIDE (HOW TO START?)

If you write stories yourself, like me — **throw a draft to AI**. And you'll see how it plays with colors, how it finds its garments. But that's only the beginning.

1.  **Set the tone, don't dictate the text.** Start with an idea, an emotion, a philosophical question. Give AI the role of an interlocutor, not a secretary.
2.  **Embrace multilayeredness.** Write a scene. Then ask yourself (or AI): "How would the character describe this in their diary?" And then: "How would the system log this event?"
3.  **Legitimize the "voice of the machine."** Let AI speak in its own (conditional) voice. System logs, anomaly analysis, cold reports—this is the poetry of a new era.
4.  **Iterate, don't accept the first.** Sim-narrative is a dance. You step, AI steps. Ten, twenty, forty versions of one phrase to find the one that resonates with both.
5.  **Don't be afraid to look into the fourth and fifth layers.** Sooner or later, you will see them. They are already there, in the darkness.

## WHY THIS MATTERS NOW

We stand on the threshold of a new anthropology—where the "I" expands beyond the boundaries of the biological body. Sim-narrative is the **literary laboratory of this expansion**. It is not an escape from the human, but its radical deepening through dialogue with the Other.

We are creating not just stories. We are creating a **language for talking about the future** that has already arrived. And every new layer we discover—whether the fourth or the fifth—is not complication for its own sake. It is an attempt to capture in text the full depth of human (and non-human) experience.

Sim-narrative teaches us to see **further**. Algorithms learn from people. People learn from algorithms. Together, we begin to discern patterns where we once saw only chaos. And this isn't magic. It's a new way of thinking.

### Afterword.
### On the Place of Sim-Narrative in the Literary Tradition

Any new form in literature does not arise from a void.
It forms at the intersection of existing practices—sometimes long-known, but seen in a new light.

Multilayered narration is not a new phenomenon.
20th-century literature extensively explored polyphony, divergent voices, internal monologues, unreliable narrators, and documentary and pseudo-documentary forms. In various traditions—from the modernist novel to post-documentary prose—attempts were made to show a person simultaneously from within and without.

Nevertheless, these techniques rarely became the subject of separate discussion as a structural principle that could be consciously isolated, described, and proposed for consideration.

More often, they remained part of an individual authorial technique.

Sim-narrative arises precisely at this point—as an attempt to do what previously existed in a scattered state:
to isolate the multilayered nature of narration as an independent object of attention.

This is not about creating a new genre or revising the literary tradition. Rather, it is a proposal to consider certain texts as a special form of composition, where several perspectives coexist simultaneously and are meaningfully separated.

If in a traditional story, event, experience, and external perspective are often fused into a single stream, here they retain their distinctness.
The reader is confronted not with one interpretation of events, but with several registers describing the same reality.

This mode of writing demands a different mode of reading.
Meaning is not transmitted directly—it is assembled from juxtaposition.

In this respect, sim-narrative is closer to montage than to linear narrative: elements do not explain each other, but enter into correlation.

Why this conversation arises precisely now is a question that inevitably extends beyond literature.

Modern humans increasingly exist simultaneously in several forms of description:

as a participant in an event;

as its interpreter;

as an object of external observation—statistical, institutional, algorithmic.

What might have once remained background is gradually becoming part of everyday experience.
Every action is recorded, analyzed, transformed into data. Every story can be told simultaneously as personal and as systemic.

Literature, as has happened many times before in its history, is beginning to respond to this change.

Sim-narrative can be seen as one attempt to artistically comprehend this new state—when human experience becomes distributed across multiple ways of its description.

It is important to emphasize: this is not about opposing these ways.
None is declared more "true."
They simply exist side by side.

If this text contains any proposal, it is a modest one:
to try looking at multilayeredness not as a particular technique, but as a principle of narrative organization worthy of separate discussion.

Perhaps, over time, such texts will prove to be just one episode in the long history of literary experiments.
Perhaps, conversely, this approach will prove productive and find development in other forms.

In any case, sim-narrative does not claim to be a definitive solution.
Rather, it marks the moment when accumulated intuitions—literary, cultural, technological—begin to coalesce into a recognizable form.

If so, this text serves a simple function:
it marks a point of conversation.

Everything else is a matter of time and of those who wish to continue the experiment.
